# 📊 Power Rating System — Premier League Form Analysis

## What is Power Rating?

The **Power Rating** is a synthetic metric designed to quantify a football team's recent form. It is calculated based on the last 5 matches (home for home teams, away for away teams), taking into account:
- Average points per match
- Average expected goals (xG)
- Average strength of opponents (based on table position)
- Number of dominant wins (victories by 3+ goals)

The formula weights these elements to create a composite score that reflects both effectiveness and context.

## How to interpret the Power Rating?

| Power Rating       | Interpretation                 | Description                                                                          |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **≥ +2.5**         | **Elite**                      | Outstanding form, frequent dominant wins, performing at peak level.                 |
| **+1.5 to +2.49**  | **Very Strong Form**           | Clearly better than average, regularly wins and dominates weaker teams.             |
| **+0.5 to +1.49**  | **Good Form**                  | Better than opponents on average, consistent form, not always dominant.             |
| **-0.49 to +0.49** | **Average Form**               | Comparable to opponents, balanced performances, anything can happen.                |
| **-0.5 to -1.49**  | **Poor Form**                  | Frequently loses or drops points, vulnerable to better teams.                       |
| **-1.5 to -2.49**  | **Very Poor Form**             | Regular defeats, low scoring, weak defense, unable to control matches.              |
| **≤ -2.5**         | **Crisis / Very Weak**         | Team in serious trouble, heavy losses, extremely weak performance.                  |

## Example Use

If Team A has a Power Rating of **+1.78** and Team B has **-0.92**, it strongly suggests that Team A is in significantly better form, especially if they are at home.

You can use this rating:
- To identify top-form teams and those in crisis.
- As a comparative factor for betting, predictions, or analysis.
- To detect possible upsets when a low-rated team suddenly performs well.
